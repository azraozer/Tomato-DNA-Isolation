## 🧪 Experiment Summary
In this experiment, genomic DNA that is macroscopically observable was isolated from fresh tomato tissue using materials found at home.

## 🛠 Materials
- **Biological Sample:** Fresh tomato tissue.
- **Lysis Solution:** 100 ml distilled water + 15 ml detergent + 5 g NaCl.
- **Precipitation:** Chilled 80% alcohol (Cologne) - *Stabilized for 9 hours at low temperature.*
- **Equipment:** Resealable bag, coffee filter/cheesecloth, glass beaker, stirring rod.

# 🔬 Procedure (Uygulama)

### 1. Buffer Preparation
Created a lysis solution (100ml water, 15ml detergent, 5g salt). Stirred slowly for 2 minutes to ensure homogeneity without creating excessive foam.

### 2. Mechanical Disruption
Chopped the tomato and placed it in a resealable bag. Removed all air and crushed by hand for 6 minutes until it reached a liquid state.

### 3. Chemical Lysis & Incubation
Added the lysis buffer to the tomato homogenate. Mixed gently for 10 minutes. 
> *Observation:* At the 2nd minute, I noticed foaming and intentionally reduced the mixing speed to maintain sample integrity.

### 4. Filtration & Precipitation
Filtered the mixture through cheesecloth into a beaker (approx. 1 min). Carefully added the chilled alcohol along the side of the glass to create a distinct interface.

---

## 📊 Results 

After approximately 15 minutes, white fibrous structures became fully visible at the alcohol interface.
